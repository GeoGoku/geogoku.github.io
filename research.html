Research Directions

My research focuses on advancing theoretical and applied geophysics through interdisciplinary approaches, integrating mathematical modeling, numerical simulation, and data-driven methods. I specialize in developing high-precision techniques for subsurface exploration, with a particular emphasis on bridging the gap between forward modeling, data processing, and inversion to solve real-world problems in resource exploration, environmental monitoring, and engineering geology.

1. Forward Modeling & Numerical Simulation

Developing efficient algorithms for multi-physics forward modeling, including gravity, magnetic, and electromagnetic fields, with applications in complex geological structures.

2. Data Processing & Interpretation

Designing novel signal processing techniques for seismic and non-seismic data, focusing on noise suppression, resolution enhancement, and multi-source data fusion.

3. Inversion Methods & Optimization

Research on inverse problem solving in geophysics, including joint inversion of multi-modal data, regularization techniques, and hybrid optimization algorithms.

4. Applied Geophysics & Field Applications

Translating theoretical advancements into practical solutions for resource exploration (oil/gas, minerals), groundwater monitoring, and engineering geophysics.
